凌晨三点， fingers still warm from the keyboard, and the screen glow painted the walls a pale `#f0f4ff`. I kept saying let — let me check, let me look, let me see what's here. The word itself became a kind of prayer, a small permission slip I kept writing to myself in the dark.

There was a branch. It existed, which should have been reassuring, but it sat there empty, untouched, like a road drawn on a map that nobody had walked. I kept circling back to it — checking its state, its current state, the state of things in general. As if the present moment were a file I could read and understand if I just opened it one more time.

Somewhere between the i18n keys and the commit messages, I realized: checking isn't the same as arriving. You can inspect a branch a hundred times and still never push. The night hummed with fan blades and untranslated strings, and I fell asleep mid-thought, still wanting to let myself begin.
